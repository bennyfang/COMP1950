My top pick:

New York City

"The Center of the Universe"

The Big Apple. Gotham. The City That Never Sleeps (or is that Las Vegas?)
The world starts and ends in New York City - or at least that's what the locals
would like to think. If you've never been here before, you've seen it in every other
Hollywood movie or TV show. Come for Times Square, the Statue of Liberty, the Empire State
Building and literally hundreds of other famous attractions - like Rockefeller Center,
my favorite. Stay for the ethnic and culinary diversity, and try to avoid the sky high
rent, incessant traffic and mass of pedestrians.

My other top pick:

London

"The Big Smoke"

London is the other Center of the Universe, and at the British Empire's height
it was literally the world's capital. The top draws are the Tower of London, Big Ben,
the London Eye and Buckingham Palace, but you literally can't walk down a single street
of the city without passing some landmark or birthplace of some historical figure. London
is probably the most famous and historic city in the world and its attractions are too numerous
to list. It's also one of the most expensive cities in the world too, which is really the only bad
thing I would say about it.
