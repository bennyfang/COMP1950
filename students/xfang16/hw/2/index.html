Introduction to Toronto, Canada

I have lived in Toronto for 5 years during my studies in University of Toronto. It's a pretty big city in Toronto and there are lots of things that I liked and disliked. I loved how there is many place to visit in Toronto downtown such as its distillery district and different cultural towns including Little Italy, Little Portugal and China Town. There is also a very diverse multiculturalism in Toronto where you will see people from many more areas than Vancouver, especially people from the Middle East. On the other hand, the winter of Toronto is not someone whose origin is Vancouver would like - getting -20°C as lowest temperature would not be uncommon in Toronto. Personally for me, the lack of seafood and Japanese food compared to Vancouver is also a setback for me that caused me to eventually move back to Vancouver.

Introduction to Tokyo, Japan

I have been to Tokyo for a total of 3 times during my 3 visits to Japan. It is the biggest city in Japan and it's certainly an exciting city to visit with its size and fast pace. I like the fact that Tokyo accomidates tourists very well and has famous attractions such as the Asakusa shrine and Gundam robot at Odaiba area that most tourists visit. Also, food tastes better and is cheaper than many Japanese restaurants in Canada, and public transit as well as access to convenient store and 24 hour restaurants are much better. However, as a metropolis, Tokyo certainly has its share of downsides - crowded trains and subway stations, long line-ups to restaurants and stores, and the amount of squeezing you would have to do as a participant of a popular event is almost unbearable. If you are claustrophobic you would certainly have a hard time there!


If you ever visit Tokyo I definitely recommend you to try the conveyor belt sushi. It's cheap and delicious sushi served on a moving belt and it would be an interesting experience for someone who never had sushi in this fashion!
